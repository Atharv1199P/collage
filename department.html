Departments

1. Computer Science

The Computer Science and Engineering (CSE) department focuses on the study of computers and computing, encompassing both theoretical foundations and practical applications. It covers areas like computer architecture, programming languages, software development, and computational science, with specializations in fields like AI, data science, and cybersecurity. CSE graduates find diverse career opportunities in software development, data science, AI, cybersecurity, and more.

2. Civil engineering

The civil department, or civil engineering department, is a branch of engineering focused on the design, construction, and maintenance of infrastructure, buildings, and public works. It encompasses a wide range of areas, including structural engineering, transportation, water resources, environmental engineering, and construction management. Civil engineers play a crucial role in shaping our built environment and addressing societal needs related to infrastructure and the natural environment.

3. Electronics and Telecommunication (E&TC) department

The Electronics and Telecommunication (E&TC) department focuses on the design, development, and application of electronic devices, circuits, and systems, particularly in the realm of communication. This field encompasses both the hardware and software aspects of electronic systems, with a strong emphasis on communication technologies.

4. Electrical engineering

Electrical engineering is an engineering discipline concerned with the study, design, and application of equipment, devices, and systems that use electricity, electronics, and electromagnetism. It emerged as an identifiable occupation in the latter half of the 19th century after the commercialization of the electric telegraph, the telephone, and electrical power generation, distribution, and use.

5. Mechanical engineering

The mechanical engineering department focuses on the design, analysis, manufacturing, and maintenance of mechanical systems. It involves applying principles of physics and mathematics, along with materials science, to create and improve various machines and devices. This field has broad applications, including robotics, aerospace, automotive, and more.
